Translate the world gold price into dong, adjust for the exchange rate, and you
should land on Vietnam's domestic price. You don't. SJC bars trade *above* import
parity by a margin the central bank treats as macro-relevant — in early 2026,
roughly **11–16% of the price of gold itself.**

We decompose that gap into three observable terms:

> **SJC = κ × Gold(USD) × USD/VND + Wₜ**

The first two are mechanical translation. The third — **Wₜ, the domestic wedge** —
is the structurally persistent premium that parity math leaves unexplained, and
it is what this report quantifies.

**The full report covers:**

- a worked 26 January 2026 decomposition — global gold ≈ $5,000/oz, USD/VND at 26,142, an implied wedge of ~19.4M VND/tael;
- why Decree 24–era supply rigidity makes the domestic supply curve vertical, and why the premium attaches to *bars*, not rings;
- how household "goldization" turns scarcity into a self-reinforcing loop — and the conditions that make the wedge compress.

Vietnam's gold premium is not a mispricing or a cultural quirk. It is the
predictable output of a globally priced asset forced through a domestically
constrained, thin-liquidity market.
