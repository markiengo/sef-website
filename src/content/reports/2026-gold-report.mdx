## Executive Summary

Vietnam's domestic gold price, most clearly reflected through Saigon Jewellery
Company (SJC) bars, has been driven by two observable betas: the international
USD gold cycle and the USD/VND exchange rate. These two explain the direction
of the move across the long window, accounting for a meaningful share of the
baseline appreciation.

However, neither explains the feature that repeatedly makes Vietnam's gold
market look anomalous relative to peers: a **persistent, regulated domestic
wedge** that can widen nonlinearly in times of stress. In a bullion market
where supply and arbitrage are structurally controlled, the wedge behaves like a
convenience-yield premium on deliverable, verifiable bars — in situations where:

- households' demand for monetary substitutes rises,
- substitution of gold form degrades (bars vs. rings), or
- intermediaries reprice inventory risk.

Vietnam's bullion market was historically shaped by Decree 24–era supply
rigidity, with episodic quantity injections via auctions and discrete sales
rather than continuous market-making, along with structurally thin two-way
liquidity. Demand shocks therefore clear primarily through the domestic gold
*price* rather than through quantity adjustment. In periods of stress,
expectations and positioning intensify this dynamic, generating a
self-reinforcing "scarcity + expectations" feedback loop.

This report formalises the framework around **three observable components**:

1. **Global gold price** — the USD benchmark
2. **USD/VND** — the exchange-rate translation layer
3. **The domestic wedge (Wₜ)** — the structurally persistent premium

## Key Quantitative Framework

### Price-Level Decomposition

The domestic SJC price is modelled as import-parity plus a domestic premium:

> **SJC price = κ × G(t) × FX(t) + Wₜ**

Where:
- **κ ≈ 1.2057** (unit conversion: troy ounce → Vietnamese tael, 37.5 g / 31.1035 g)
- **G(t)** = global gold price in USD/oz
- **FX(t)** = USD/VND exchange rate
- **Wₜ** = the domestic wedge (taxes, scarcity rents, risk premia, policy premium)

### Worked Example (26 January 2026)

| Input | Value |
|-------|-------|
| Global gold price | ~5,000 USD/oz |
| USD/VND | 26,142 |
| Import parity | ~157.6M VND/tael |
| SJC domestic sell | ~177.0M VND/tael |
| **Wedge (Wₜ)** | **~19.4M VND/tael (~11–16% of price)** |

## Why SJC Outruns World Benchmarks

In a frictionless environment, a globally priced commodity like gold should
obey a loose parity condition once you translate units and currency. Any large
deviation should self-correct: if domestic prices run rich, metal flows in.
Yet SJC prices repeatedly trade above import parity by an economically large
margin that the State Bank of Vietnam (SBV) treats as macro-relevant.

**Two structural reasons explain persistence:**

**1. Supply does not respond elastically.** When people suddenly want more SJC
bars, there is no automatic flow of new supply. The domestic supply curve
becomes effectively vertical under Decree 24–era constraints, so demand shocks
load entirely onto price. The 2024 SBV gold bar auctions — the first in roughly
eleven years — are direct evidence that the market was not generating an elastic
supply response at prevailing prices.

**2. SJC is product-segmented.** The wedge attaches disproportionately to SJC
bars versus rings or jewellery because bars carry superior *deliverability,
standardisation, and resale liquidity*. In other words, the premium is not a
generic cultural preference — it is a microstructure and policy outcome.

## The Wedge's Structural Channels

### Policy-Induced Supply Constraints

Decree 24 concentrated gold bar production under government control. Supply
becomes *policy-elastic* rather than price-elastic: when demand accelerates,
the adjustment loads onto the wedge (Wₜ), not onto quantity.

Decree 232/2025 is a structural break, signalling an intent to broaden the
production perimeter. But markets respond to *implementation certainty*, not
intent — and implementation uncertainty itself becomes a wedge creator during
transition windows.

### "Goldization" and the Hoarding Loop

For a large share of Vietnamese households, physical SJC bars function as a
**monetary asset**: a portable, settlement-ready store of value outside the
banking system. This demand is reflexive:

- Substitution across gold forms is imperfect under stress (rings ≠ bars for
  resale certainty)
- Expectations convert scarcity into convexity: if agents believe supply will
  remain tight, holding SJC resembles an asset with a convenience yield *plus*
  an embedded policy option
- A self-reinforcing loop can form: higher prices → reduced seller willingness
  → tighter supply → higher prices

### FX Pass-Through: Two Channels

USD/VND enters the decomposition through two distinct mechanisms:

1. **Mechanical translation** — even under full arbitrage, VND gold returns
   inherit USD gold returns plus the change in exchange rate
2. **Macro-hedge channel** — gold demand is sensitive to the *distribution* of
   future FX outcomes. When tail FX risks rise, the convenience-yield component
   of holding SJC rises, which can lift Wₜ even before spot depreciation occurs

## Conclusion

The SJC wedge is not a mysterious mispricing. It is the **predictable outcome**
of a globally priced asset trading through a domestically constrained,
compliance-sensitive, thin-liquidity market — where the wedge is the price of
immediacy and certainty under constraint.

Closing Wₜ requires not only supply injections but also durable market-making
conditions. When the supply environment loosens or market-making depth improves,
the premium can compress quickly even if global gold remains high — the
asymmetry runs both ways.
