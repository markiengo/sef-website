**Rating: Overweight · Target Price: VND 115,000 · Current Price: VND 92,100 · Upside: 24.9%**

We issue an **Overweight** rating for FPT Corporation (HOSE: FPT) with a target price of
VND 115,000, representing 24.9% upside from the current price. FPT's digital transformation
franchise — exported across 30 countries — gives it a competitive moat that domestic
technology peers cannot replicate.

---

## Investment Thesis

**Positive macroeconomic outlook despite tariff headwinds.** Software services and solutions
are not subject to the same tariff pressures facing physical goods exporters. The Vietnamese
IT industry is backed by government initiatives (resolutions 57-NQ/TW, 59-NQ/TW, 66-NQ/TW,
68-NQ/TW) that directly benefit FPT through public-private partnerships, including
collaboration with the National Innovation Center (NIC) and a contract with NVIDIA to cement
FPT as a major regional software provider.

**Diverse, growing revenue streams.** In 2024, FPT reported revenue of VND 62,849 billion
and EBIT of VND 11,070 billion, with a 28.5% ROE and 14.4% ROA. IT services contributed
62.2% of total revenue, telecommunications 28.0%, with the remainder in education and other
services. The company employs 54,646 workers, deploys 250 distinct platforms and solutions,
and operates 2 AI factories in Vietnam and Japan.

**Strategic international positioning.** FPT's most profitable segment — digital
transformation consulting — is exported to clients in 30 countries, creating a competitive
moat among domestic technology companies that shields FPT's finances from an oversaturated
local market. This international orientation diversifies the revenue base beyond Vietnam's
domestic IT demand cycle.

**Education platform with structural growth.** FPT's education division enrolls over 152,000
students across 28 provinces at K-12 through postgraduate levels, positioning FPT to capture
the long-run return on Vietnam's technology talent pipeline — and to benefit as those
graduates flow into the broader digital economy.

## Key Risks

The principal risks to the thesis are: execution risk on NVIDIA and government-mandated
contracts; margin compression as FPT competes in higher-cost international markets;
and broader macroeconomic deceleration in key export markets (Japan, the United States,
and Europe account for the largest shares of international IT services revenue).

Southeast Asia's digital economy generated USD 89 billion in revenue in 2024, with AI
infrastructure attracting over USD 30 billion in committed investment in the first half of
the year alone. Vietnam's domestic IT market, estimated at approximately USD 8 billion, is
projected to grow at a 9.34% CAGR through 2029.

FPT is the clearest Vietnamese-listed proxy for this structural shift.
