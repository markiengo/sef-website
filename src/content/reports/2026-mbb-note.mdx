Military Commercial Joint Stock Bank (HoSE: MBB) was established in 1994 with the initial
mandate of serving financial needs within Vietnam's military enterprise sector. Over three
decades of expansion, MBB has evolved into one of Vietnam's larger joint-stock commercial
banks by total assets, with a national network covering 24 provinces, a head office in
Hanoi, and an international branch in Laos.

MBB listed its shares on the Ho Chi Minh Stock Exchange on 1 November 2011 — a milestone
that formalised the bank's transition toward greater transparency and market accountability,
and opened its equity to a broader investor base.

---

## Business Structure

MBB operates as a financial conglomerate anchored by the parent bank, with five subsidiaries
spanning insurance, securities brokerage, real estate, and consumer finance. This
diversification provides earnings resilience across credit cycles and allows the group to
capture fee income from adjacent financial services — a structural advantage shared by the
larger state-linked banks but less common among pure-play joint-stock peers.

The bank has consistently maintained the State Bank of Vietnam's highest operational rating
(Category A) across its recent assessment history, reflecting compliance with capital
adequacy and prudential standards.

---

## Sector Context: Vietnamese Banking in 2026

The Vietnamese banking sector enters 2026 with a dual dynamic. Credit growth remains a
critical transmission mechanism for economic expansion — the government and SBV continue to
rely on bank lending to drive infrastructure-adjacent investment and household consumption.
At the same time, elevated non-performing loan levels (particularly in real estate and
construction) and rising loan-to-deposit ratios signal growing financial stability risks.

Household savers have diversified away from traditional bank deposits toward equities, gold,
and digital assets, tightening the funding base. The SBV's policy response — balancing
USD/VND stability against maintaining credit supply — has leaned on administrative tools
rather than outright rate moves, keeping the interest rate environment in a managed range
rather than a tightening cycle.

MBB's military-affiliated depositor base and enterprise client network provide a degree of
funding stability not fully captured in headline sector metrics.

## Key Considerations

For investors evaluating MBB against sector peers, the critical variables are: (1) loan
book composition and real estate/construction exposure relative to sector averages; (2) NIM
trajectory as funding costs adjust to deposit competition from alternative assets; and
(3) capital adequacy headroom for credit expansion in line with government growth targets.
