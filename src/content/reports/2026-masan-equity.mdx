Masan Group (HoSE: MSN) is not a conglomerate in the traditional sense. It is a vertically
integrated consumer ecosystem that owns the brands Vietnamese households buy, the stores
they shop in, and the supply chain that delivers both. Understanding Masan is, in large part,
understanding the structural shift in how Vietnam's growing middle class consumes.

---

## Business Overview

Masan's revenue engine across its key operating segments for the first nine months of 2025
reached VND 58,376 billion. The four core contributors:

- **WinCommerce (WCM):** VND 28,459 billion — 4,146 WinMart and WinMart+ stores, the
  largest modern retail network in Vietnam by outlet count, with WinMart+ expanding
  aggressively into rural markets (+47% rural store growth).
- **Masan Consumer (MCH):** VND 21,281 billion — the FMCG powerhouse behind Chin-su,
  Nam Ngu fish sauce, and Omachi noodles. Brands exported to 26 countries, including
  shelf placement at Costco US and Korea.
- **Masan MEATLife (MML):** VND 6,794 billion — farm-to-retail chilled meat, targeting
  Vietnam's urban middle class with food-safety branding.
- **Masan High-Tech Materials (MHT):** VND 5,048 billion (like-for-like) — tungsten
  processing, insulated from most consumer macro risks.

## The Modern Trade Thesis

Vietnam's modern trade penetration stands at approximately 27% of retail in 2025, on track
for 35% by 2030 — the fastest modernisation trajectory in ASEAN. Total retail sales for the
first nine months of 2025 reached VND 5.176 quadrillion, up 9.5% year-on-year.

Final consumption rose 8.1% YoY in the same period and accounted for 74% of GDP growth.
With consumption as the dominant driver of Vietnam's economic expansion, retail and FMCG
companies with broad product coverage and strong distribution benefit disproportionately
versus export-dependent sectors.

The WiN membership program, with approximately 11 million subscribers, enables targeted
geographic and demographic marketing and supports Masan's "Point of Life" ecosystem
strategy — the ambition to serve a Vietnamese consumer's entire daily consumption need
through integrated touchpoints.

## Key Risks

Masan's complexity introduces concentration and governance risks. In 2025, entities within
the group were found to have misused VND 1,296 billion in bond proceeds for debt
restructuring — triggering tighter regulatory scrutiny. The 2025 tax reform disrupted
inventory management and forced a temporary destocking cycle. Competition from Chinese
imports (notably Mixue in beverages), multinational FMCG giants (Nestlé, Unilever), and
the rapid growth of e-commerce (34%+ YoY order growth on major platforms) all create
pricing and distribution pressure.

Vietnam's Generation Z — representing approximately 40% of the consumer market by 2030 —
prioritise health, wellness, and convenience over legacy FMCG formats, requiring continuous
product innovation.

Masan's FY 2025 guidance targets revenue of approximately VND 86 trillion through global
retail penetration and supply-chain optimisation.
